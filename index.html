Design Beginnings


Design Fuego

Design Fuego was a great way to get our feet wet in the digital design realm. It allowed us to understand how html and web design worked without overwhelming the students that had no background in the material. It even gave the students an idea on their perception of what "good design" is which guided the next couple of projects in the course.

Logo A/B/C

Personally, I interpreted the A/B/C project very differently to my peers. Instead of making a logo that gave the audience a sense of who I am, I instead made 3 logos that represented my interests in the world as if thet were real companies. Regardles, I thoroughly enjoyed the process of using canva as well as my first time using adobe. It was a creative awakening seeing all the tools that I had at my disposal.

UX Testing Logo Project

The UX research was an elightening way to get feedback without overwhelming the designers with critiques. Since design is very subjective, it also allowed us to keep in mind the goals we want with our logos rather than only change what our participants wanted us to. Personally, I definetly took some advice with a grain of salt, and others, I kept in mind. My final logo represented me much more than my preliminary designs, and I am glad to have gotten the feedback I did.

Cartoon Portrait

The Cartoon design was one the most challenging for me thus far. It took me a while to get the hang of using adobe and layering. It doesn't help that I'm not the most creative individual, so figuring out what exactly I wanted to include and not include was difficult. When I have the time later on in the term, I plan on tweaking it to be more realisitic and less "creepy".

Good Design

Our last project of design beginnings was our "good design" piece. I very much love this type of graphic design as it's clean and neat without being boring. I chose my artwork as one that was important to me as an individual. It portrayed an important matter as well as made it aesthetically pleasing. It had all the elements of proximity, alignment, and color choice that was discussed in class, making it an overall solid piece of work.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

In my experience as a student of computational biology, this role deeply intertwines the process of making and problem-solving. When developing computational tools or analyzing biological data, the rhetoric we use influences how we communicate findings and their significance, both to scientific and public audiences. Technology serves as the medium through which we explore these insights, but ethical considerations, especially around data privacy and the impact on human health, must guide every decision.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Principles like simplicity, balance, and user-centered design guide how I approach both technical systems and patient care. By focusing on clarity and usability, I ensure that my designs—whether digital tools or real-life actions—are effective, efficient, and ethical

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

I've found that collaborating with peers through critiques and brainstorming help refine concepts and introduces new perspectives that I wouldn't have considered alone. Whether in designing or working in bioinformatics, this engagement fosters innovation and encourages solutions that are both practical and thoughtfully constructed.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Practicing revision across media forms in my role as a student has taught me how different approaches can enhance a project’s clarity and impact. Whether revising a written assignment or redesigning a visual presentation, adapting content across formats allows for better communication and engagement.

5. Learn to present your work.

Through presentations in class, Ive discovered the importance of tailoring content to the audience and clearly articulating the thought process behind a project. Whether sharing design ideas or research findings, presenting allows me to showcase my work while receiving valuable feedback for improvement.

Short Form


Two-page Spread

Building more using InDesign, I found my artistic side coming out with this project. I tried to find a cover that was fun and hip but also had the color scheme I desired. I really enjoyed the art I chose and I think it fits my personality very well.

Graphic Design Resume

Deciding what to portray in my resume came easier to me than deciding how I wanted to lay it out in InDesign. I knew that the organizational side of me wanted a grid like structure, so that's what I did, and I am very proud of how it turned out.

Advertisement

I am one of the few raiders fans left in this country, so the minute the project was pitched in class, I knew I wanted to do something that showed my *unfortunate* pride in my team. Building more in Indesign, I've gotten much more comfortable with the tools. I imported some fonts to make it more visually appealing and I think it turned out better than expected.
