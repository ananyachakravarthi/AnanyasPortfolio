Design Beginnings


Design Fuego

Design Fuego was a great way to get our feet wet in the digital design realm. It allowed us to understand how html and web design worked without overwhelming the students that had no background in the material. It even gave the students an idea on their perception of what "good design" is which guided the next couple of projects in the course.

Logo A/B/C

Personally, I interpreted the A/B/C project very differently to my peers. Instead of making a logo that gave the audience a sense of who I am, I instead made 3 logos that represented my interests in the world as if thet were real companies. Regardles, I thoroughly enjoyed the process of using canva as well as my first time using adobe. It was a creative awakening seeing all the tools that I had at my disposal.

UX Testing Logo Project

The UX research was an elightening way to get feedback without overwhelming the designers with critiques. Since design is very subjective, it also allowed us to keep in mind the goals we want with our logos rather than only change what our participants wanted us to. Personally, I definetly took some advice with a grain of salt, and others, I kept in mind. My final logo represented me much more than my preliminary designs, and I am glad to have gotten the feedback I did.

Cartoon Portrait

The Cartoon design was one the most challenging for me thus far. It took me a while to get the hang of using adobe and layering. It doesn't help that I'm not the most creative individual, so figuring out what exactly I wanted to include and not include was difficult. When I have the time later on in the term, I plan on tweaking it to be more realisitic and less "creepy".

Good Design

Our last project of design beginnings was our "good design" piece. I very much love this type of graphic design as it's clean and neat without being boring. I chose my artwork as one that was important to me as an individual. It portrayed an important matter as well as made it aesthetically pleasing. It had all the elements of proximity, alignment, and color choice that was discussed in class, making it an overall solid piece of work.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

Rhetoric allows me to convey complex ideas clearly, whether in communicating scientific insights, providing critical information to patients, or visually representing themes like sustainability, as seen in my design projects, especially in my "good design". Technology serves as a bridge, enabling medical and biological advances through computational tools and enriching my design capabilities. Ethics ensures that my work, from handling genomic data to respecting patient autonomy and promoting truthful design, aligns with values of integrity and respect.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

I leverage design principles to enhance communication, clarity, and impact across diverse fields. In the "Pre-hospital care" logo, I used simplicity and symbolism to instantly convey the compassion and support central to emergency medical services—key elements in a field where clear, quick recognition is essential. Similarly, in the "Locally Sourced" project, I applied balance, typography hierarchy, and negative space to create a calm, organized layout that reinforces the themes of sustainability and authenticity, appealing to viewers values.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

As a designer, I lead creative workshops where I facilitate brainstorming sessions, gather diverse perspectives, and refine design concepts collaboratively, as demonstrated in my projects, especially my Design Fuego and even my cartoon. These experiences across different fields have honed my ability to adapt to various group dynamics, contribute meaningfully to multidisciplinary teams, and leverage each workshop opportunity to achieve impactful and cohesive results.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Practicing revision across media forms in my role as a student has taught me how different approaches can enhance a project’s clarity and impact. Whether revising a written assignment or redesigning a visual presentation, adapting content across formats allows for better communication and engagement. I got a ton of well deserved feedback for my cartoon project and I tweaked a lot of small issues that showed.

5. Learn to present your work.

Through presentations in class, Ive discovered the importance of tailoring content to the audience and clearly articulating the thought process behind a project. Whether sharing design ideas or research findings, presenting allows me to showcase my work while receiving valuable feedback for improvement. With Design Fuego, I was a little concerned to show the class, however, as the class went on, the more comfortable I got.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

The projects I've made in my short form demonstrate how rhetoric and ethics play a vital role in design, especially for communication purposes. In the two page spread, for instance, rhetoric is used to disrupt traditional views, creating a visually and emotionally engaging ad that speaks to a rebellious, younger demographic. Meanwhile, ethics are considered subtly in design choices, ensuring the ad does not alienate traditional wine audiences while appealing to new customers.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Each design leverages core design principles effectively. The EMS recruitment poster uses hierarchy, contrast, and alignment to communicate information clearly, helping readers quickly understand job benefits and application instructions. For example, the bolded job titles and benefits package highlight key details, drawing immediate attention. The resume leverages alignment, contrast, and spacing effectively to guide the reader’s eye and emphasize different sections (like "Experience" and "Skills"). The design is clean and professional, with a simple monochromatic color scheme that reflects a serious, academic tone, aligning with my career goals.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

In previous iterations of the Raiders poster, I made several changes to improve its visual impact and alignment with the team’s branding. Initially, the design had less contrast and didn’t fully capture the bold, intense vibe that represents Raiders culture. After receiving feedback, I adjusted the color balance and added stronger contrasts to emphasize the “Silver & Black” theme, aligning more closely with the Raiders' iconic brand.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

The designs exhibit skills that can be applied across different media. For instance, the EMS recruitment ad could transition to a digital format for online recruitment, incorporating animation for additional engagement. Similarly, the wine advertisement could be adapted into an interactive webpage or social media format, where movement could be enhanced through animation to simulate the wine pouring effect.

5. Learn to present your work.

Presenting my resume, especially would involve explaining how the layout and content reflect my skills and goals as a computational biology student, aiming to establish a strong, professional image aligned with your career aspirations. By clearly articulating these choices, I convey my understanding of the design’s purpose and my ability to create effective, audience-tailored work.
